Biography

Savitri Jindal ranks third in India with approximately $35.5 billion in January 2026. As non-executive chairperson of the OP Jindal Group, she oversees one of India's largest steel and power conglomerates. After her husband's tragic death in 2005, she took leadership of the family business and successfully guided its expansion into new sectors while maintaining family unity across diversified businesses run by her four sons.

Frequently Asked Questions About Savitri Jindal

How did Savitri Jindal become the richest woman in India?

Jindal inherited the OP Jindal Group after her husband's death in 2005 and successfully managed the transition while expanding the business. Her strategic leadership and family governance model allowed the group to grow significantly under her sons' management.

How many companies do the Jindal family control?

The Jindal family controls over 15 major companies across steel, power, infrastructure, cement, and renewable energy. Key listed entities include JSW Steel, Jindal Steel & Power, JSW Energy, and JSW Infrastructure.

What is Savitri Jindal's educational background?

Savitri Jindal completed her education in Haryana and was primarily involved in family and social work before taking over business leadership. She has since gained extensive business experience through decades of involvement in the family enterprise.

How does the Jindal family manage their businesses?

Each of Savitri Jindal's four sons manages different segments: Sajjan Jindal (JSW Group), Prithviraj Jindal (Jindal SAW), Ratan Jindal (Jindal Stainless), and Naveen Jindal (Jindal Power). Savitri serves as non-executive chairperson overseeing the entire group.

What is Jindal's political involvement?

Savitri Jindal served as a Minister in the Haryana government from 2005-2014 and was a Member of Legislative Assembly. She has since focused primarily on business while maintaining political connections through her son Naveen Jindal, who is an active politician.
