Daily Change: +2.5%

Biography

Elon Musk ends 2025 as the world’s wealthiest person with approximately $726.3 billion. His wealth primarily comes from Tesla’s continued dominance in electric vehicles and energy storage, and SpaceX’s successful Mars mission and Starlink satellite internet service, which became profitable in 2025. Musk’s visionary leadership has accelerated renewable energy adoption and advanced space exploration, while his neural interface projects point to new frontiers in human–computer integration.

Born in Pretoria, South Africa, Musk moved to North America to pursue engineering and entrepreneurship. He has repeatedly focused on technologies with long time horizons, pairing bold vision with rapid iteration and a willingness to pursue vertical integration to control the pace of innovation.

Core Companies

  • Tesla: EVs, energy storage, and AI-driven manufacturing.
  • SpaceX: Launch services, Starship development, and Starlink.
  • Neuralink: Brain–computer interface research.
  • The Boring Company: Tunnel infrastructure and mobility.

Innovation Highlights

Strategic Priorities

  • Expanding global Starlink coverage.
  • Scaling next-gen battery chemistry.
  • Integrating AI tooling across Tesla factories.

Signature Leadership Traits

Philanthropy

In 2025, Musk donated $8 billion worth of Tesla shares to various causes including renewable energy research, science education and the XPRIZE Carbon Removal competition.

Additional giving focuses on STEM education, climate resilience, and local workforce development initiatives tied to advanced manufacturing hubs.
