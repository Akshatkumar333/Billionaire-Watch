Frequently Asked Questions About Steve Ballmer

How much Microsoft stock does Steve Ballmer own?

Ballmer owns approximately 300 million Microsoft shares, representing about 4% of the company. This makes him the largest individual shareholder after Bill Gates, despite selling billions in stock since retirement.

Why did Ballmer buy the LA Clippers?

Ballmer purchased the Clippers for $2 billion in 2014 after Donald Sterling's racism scandal. He saw undervalued potential in LA's second NBA team and wanted to compete with the Lakers in the nation's second-largest market.

What is Ballmer's management style?

Known for intense energy and passion, Ballmer's "monkey boy" dance became legendary. He focuses on sales execution, competitive intensity, and employee motivation through his famous "developers, developers, developers" speech.

How does Ballmer's philanthropy differ from Gates?

While Gates focuses on global health, Ballmer's Ballmer Group targets economic mobility in the US through data-driven approaches. His USAFacts project provides government transparency rather than direct aid.

What was Ballmer's biggest mistake at Microsoft?

Ballmer admits missing mobile revolution was his biggest mistake. He dismissed iPhone as irrelevant in 2007, saying "no chance" it would gain significant market share, while Microsoft's Windows Mobile failed to compete.
