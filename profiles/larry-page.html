Biography & Career Journey

Larry Page ranks second globally with approximately $256.9 billion net worth in 2026. His wealth comes from Alphabet (Google's parent company), which saw massive growth in AI services, cloud computing, and autonomous vehicles through Waymo in 2025.

Born in East Lansing, Michigan, Page displayed early interest in computers. He earned his Bachelor's degree from the University of Michigan and later pursued a Master's at Stanford University, where he met Sergey Brin. Together, they developed the PageRank algorithm that would become the foundation of Google.

Key Achievements:

  • Co-founded Google in 1998 with Sergey Brin
  • Served as Google's first CEO until 2001, then again from 2011-2015
  • Led the creation of Alphabet Inc. in 2015, serving as CEO until 2019
  • Pioneered Google's "moonshot" projects including self-driving cars and life sciences
  • Holds multiple patents including the PageRank algorithm patent

Frequently Asked Questions

How much of Alphabet does Larry Page own?

As of January 2026, Larry Page owns approximately 5.8% of Alphabet's outstanding shares, representing about 25 million Class A and Class C shares. This ownership accounts for approximately 85% of his total net worth.

What is Larry Page's daily income?

Based on his net worth growth of 30% in 2025-2026, Larry Page's wealth increased by approximately $59.3 billion in one year, equating to about $162 million per day or $6.8 million per hour.

How does Larry Page's wealth compare to Sergey Brin's?

In 2026, Larry Page's net worth is $256.9B compared to Sergey Brin's $237.1B. The $19.8B difference comes from Page's slightly larger initial Google ownership and different investment portfolios.

What is Larry Page's salary at Alphabet?

Since stepping down as CEO in 2019, Page receives a symbolic $1 annual salary. His compensation comes entirely from stock appreciation and dividends.

Will Larry Page become the world's richest person?

While possible, Page would need Alphabet's value to triple while Tesla/SpaceX stagnates. Most projections suggest he will remain in the top 3-5 but may not surpass Elon Musk unless there are significant shifts in both companies' trajectories.
