Frequently Asked Questions About Sergey Brin

How much of Alphabet does Sergey Brin own today?

As of January 2026, Sergey Brin owns approximately 5.5% of Alphabet's outstanding shares, representing about 80 million Class A and Class C shares. This ownership accounts for approximately 83% of his total net worth.

What is Sergey Brin's current role at Alphabet?

Although he stepped back from day-to-day operations in 2019, Brin returned in 2023 as President of Alphabet's Special Projects, focusing on AI research and moonshot technologies. He remains on the board and is actively involved in strategic decisions.

How does Brin's wealth compare to Larry Page's?

In 2026, Sergey Brin's net worth is $237.1B compared to Larry Page's $256.9B. The $19.8B difference comes from Page's slightly larger initial Google ownership and different investment strategies outside Alphabet.

What is Brin's involvement in Parkinson's research?

Following his mother's Parkinson's diagnosis, Brin has committed over $1 billion to research through the Sergey Brin Family Foundation. He carries the LRRK2 genetic mutation himself, giving him a 30-75% chance of developing Parkinson's.

What are Brin's most successful moonshot investments?

Beyond Google/Alphabet, Brin's most successful investments include flying car company Kitty Hawk ($15B valuation), quantum computing startup Rigetti ($8B valuation), and biotech firm Verily ($35B valuation).
