Biography & Career Journey

Larry Ellison holds third place globally with approximately $245 billion net worth in 2026. Oracle's transformation into a cloud and AI powerhouse drove significant wealth growth in 2025, with the company gaining market share in enterprise software and database services.

Born in New York City to an unmarried Jewish mother, Ellison was adopted by his aunt and uncle. He attended the University of Chicago but dropped out before completing his degree. After moving to California, he worked as a programmer before founding Oracle in 1977 with $2,000 of his own money.

Key Achievements:

  • Co-founded Oracle Corporation in 1977 (originally Software Development Laboratories)
  • Built Oracle into the world's leading database software company
  • Successfully navigated Oracle's transition from software licensing to cloud services
  • Served as Oracle CEO for 37 years (1977-2014)
  • Current Chief Technology Officer and Executive Chairman of Oracle
  • Major investor in Tesla and member of its board of directors

Frequently Asked Questions

How much of Oracle does Larry Ellison own?

As of January 2026, Larry Ellison owns approximately 35% of Oracle's outstanding shares, representing about 1.05 billion shares. This is one of the largest ownership stakes of any founder in a major technology company.

Why did Larry Ellison buy the island of Lanai?

Ellison purchased 98% of Lanai in 2012 for $300 million to create a model for sustainable living. He has invested over $500 million in renewable energy, organic farming, and conservation projects on the island.

How does Ellison's wealth compare to other tech founders his age?

At 81, Ellison is significantly wealthier than other tech founders from his generation. Bill Gates (age 70) has $120B, while Steve Ballmer (age 69) has $118B. Ellison's continuous involvement in Oracle and successful cloud transition explains this difference.

What is Larry Ellison's salary at Oracle?

As Executive Chairman and CTO, Ellison receives a $1 annual salary. His compensation comes entirely from stock appreciation. He has never taken a traditional salary or bonus from Oracle.

Will Oracle survive after Larry Ellison?

Oracle has developed strong leadership succession with Safra Catz as CEO. The company's institutional knowledge, patent portfolio, and customer relationships suggest it will remain dominant in enterprise software regardless of Ellison's involvement.

Legacy & Business Philosophy

Larry Ellison's impact extends beyond Oracle to influencing enterprise software, cloud computing, and corporate leadership philosophy.

Key Business Principles:

  • "Attack Weakness": Competitive strategy focusing on competitors' vulnerabilities
  • "Software is the Business": Early recognition that software, not hardware, creates value
  • "Customer Lock-in": Build products so integral to operations that switching costs are prohibitive
  • "Innovate or Die": Continuous product development even when market-leading
  • "Take Big Bets": Willingness to risk company on major technological shifts
