Biography

Shiv Nadar is an Indian billionaire businessman and philanthropist, closing 2025 with approximately $34.5 billion. Known as the founder and chairman emeritus of HCL Technologies, he transformed a hardware startup founded in a garage in 1976 into a global IT services giant with over $13.8 billion in revenue. Affectionately nicknamed "Magus" (meaning wizard), he is equally celebrated for his transformational philanthropy in education through the Shiv Nadar Foundation.

Frequently Asked Questions About Shiv Nadar

Why does Shiv Nadar hold so much cash?

Analysts attribute his ~$7.3 billion cash reserve to a conservative financial philosophy that values liquidity for flexibility, opportunity seizing, and easy deployment of philanthropic funds without needing to sell stock. It's a strategic choice for stability and control.

How much of HCL does Shiv Nadar still own?

While exact percentages shift due to ongoing succession transfers, he remains the largest shareholder. In 2025, he transferred additional controlling stakes in investment companies holding HCL shares to his daughter, Roshni.

What is the Shiv Nadar Foundation's main focus?

The foundation focuses on transformational education. Its flagship initiatives include the SSN College of Engineering, Vidyagyan leadership schools for rural scholars, and partnerships with top global universities to uplift India's educational ecosystem.

Did Shiv Nadar have a technical background?

Yes. He earned a degree in Electrical and Electronics Engineering from PSG College of Technology in Coimbatore. Interestingly, his early education was primarily in Tamil, and he became fluent in English only around age 22.

What is Nadar's leadership style known for?

He is known for decentralization and empowering leaders within HCL. The company famously hires high school graduates and trains them on the job, reflecting a belief in potential over pedigree. His succession plan with his daughter is noted for its clarity and gradual execution.
