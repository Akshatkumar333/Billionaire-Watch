Frequently Asked Questions About Amancio Ortega

How much of Inditex does Amancio Ortega own?

Ortega owns approximately 60% of Inditex through various holding companies. He remains the largest shareholder despite stepping down as chairman in 2011. The remaining 40% is publicly traded on Spanish stock exchanges.

What is Ortega's real estate strategy?

Through Pontegadea Inversiones, Ortega invests in prime commercial real estate in major global cities. The portfolio focuses on office buildings and retail spaces in London, New York, Madrid, and Miami, generating stable rental income.

Why is Ortega so private?

Ortega has given only three interviews in his life and avoids publicity. He believes "the secret of success is to appear normal" and maintains that customers, not media attention, should be the focus.

How does Zara differ from competitors?

Zara's unique advantage is speed: designs move from sketch to stores in 2 weeks vs. 6 months for competitors. This allows rapid response to trends and minimal inventory risk.

What is Ortega's management style?

Despite his wealth, Ortega maintains a modest office at Inditex headquarters, eats in the employee cafeteria, and emphasizes teamwork. His motto: "If you want to do things well, do them yourself, but surround yourself with good people."
